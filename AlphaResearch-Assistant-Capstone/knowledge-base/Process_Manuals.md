## PROCESS 1: FIRST-TIME INVESTOR ONBOARDING

### Scenario: First Time Customer Wants to Buy Stocks

**Customer:** Rajesh Kumar, Software Engineer, Age 35  
**Objective:** Invest ₹5,00,000 to build long-term wealth, understand equity market

#### Step 1: Pre-Opening Consultation (Week 1)

**What Happens:**
1. Customer calls branch or visits website
2. Relationship Manager (RM) conducts financial consultation
3. Discusses:
   - Investment objective: Long-term wealth (5+ years)
   - Risk appetite: Medium (can tolerate 15-20% volatility)
   - Time commitment: 1-2 hours weekly for monitoring
   - Existing investments: (if any)

**Recommended Products:**
- Equity delivery-based trading (T+1 settlement)
- Blue-chip stocks: HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, Reliance, TCS, ITC
- Diversification: Avoid single stock concentration
- Suggested allocation: 30% banking, 25% IT, 20% auto, 15% FMCG, 10% others

**Documentation Provided:**
- Account opening form
- KYC checklist
- Risk disclosure document
- SEBI investor charter (explains protection)
- Fee structure and charges

#### Step 2: Account Opening (Week 1-2)

**Documents Submitted:**
- PAN card, Aadhaar, utility bill (recent)
- Photograph and signature samples
- Bank statement (to confirm bank for funding)
- Income proof (salary slip for last 3 months)

**Back-Office Processing:**
- Day 1: KYC validation, PAN verification
- Day 2: Aadhaar link-up, CIBIL check
- Day 3: Demat account creation with NSDL
- Day 4: Account activation, credentials sent

**Timeline:** 4-5 business days

#### Step 3: Account Funding (Week 2)

**Process:**
1. Transfer ₹5,00,000 via NEFT from salary account
2. Bank transfer takes 1-2 hours
3. Amount credited to broker's escrow account
4. System automatically books ₹5,00,000 in trading account
5. Confirmation email sent
6. Amount available for trading immediately

**Available for Trading:**
- Full ₹5,00,000 available next trading session
- Can execute buy/sell orders without delay

#### Step 4: First Purchase - Buying Stocks (Week 2)

**RM Guidance:**
"Let's build a balanced portfolio. Suggest buying these in phases:"

**Decision:** Buy top 5 stocks (₹1,00,000 each)

**Stock Selection & Execution:**

| Stock | Quantity | Price | Value | Allocation % | Reason |
|-------|----------|-------|-------|--------------|--------|
| HDFC Bank | 40 | ₹2,500 | ₹1,00,000 | 20% | Banking major, dividend yield 2% |
| ICICI Bank | 50 | ₹2,000 | ₹1,00,000 | 20% | Banking, growth potential |
| Reliance Industries | 30 | ₹3,300 | ₹99,000 | 20% | Diversified conglomerate |
| TCS | 50 | ₹2,000 | ₹1,00,000 | 20% | IT leader, stable dividend |
| ITC | 150 | ₹680 | ₹1,02,000 | 20% | FMCG, decent dividend, value |

**Execution:**
1. Access trading terminal, place 5 market buy orders
2. Order sent to NSE within 30 milliseconds
3. Execution at ₹2,500, ₹2,000 etc. (slightly different than expected due to bid-ask spread)
4. Actual total: ₹5,01,200 (slight overshoot, within tolerance)
5. Confirmation received: "5 trades executed"

**T+1 Settlement:**
- **Trade Day (T):** 5 stock purchased, payment pending
- **Settlement Day (T+1):** 
  - Shares credited to Demat account (NSDL confirmation)
  - Cash debited: ₹5,01,200
  - Available balance: ₹0 (fully invested)
  - New portfolio value: ₹5,01,200

#### Step 5: Ongoing Management (Months 2+)

**Week 1 After Purchase:**
- Portfolio value: ₹5,10,000 (+₹9,000 or +1.8%)
- Receive holding confirmation email
- Corporate actions notification: HDFC Bank announces ₹28/share dividend (upcoming)

**Month 3 (After Dividend):**
- HDFC Bank dividend ₹28 x 40 shares = ₹1,120 received
- Cash credited to trading account
- Options: Reinvest or keep as buffer

**Month 6 (Review):**
- Portfolio value: ₹5,25,000 (+₹23,800 or +4.7% half-yearly)
- Evaluate: Are allocations still balanced?
- Decision: ICICI Bank has risen 15%, reduce exposure by selling 20 shares @ ₹2,300
- Proceeds: ₹46,000 → Redeploy into underperforming IT stocks

**Year 1 Review:**
- Portfolio value: ₹5,75,000 (+₹73,800 or +14.7% yearly)
- Tax implication: All gains are SHORT-TERM (< 1 year)
  - Tax: 30% on ₹73,800 = ₹22,140
  - Net profit: ₹51,660 (after tax)
- Recommendation: Hold HDFC/TCS long-term for indexation benefit

## PROCESS 2: INTRADAY TRADING - ACTIVE TRADER WORKFLOW

### Scenario: Active Trader Wants Daily Income Through Trading

**Trader:** Priya Sharma, Ex-Fund Manager, Age 45  
**Strategy:** Intraday trading (buy and sell same day, lock profits)  
**Capital:** ₹10,00,000

#### Pre-Market (9:00 AM - 9:15 AM)

**Activities:**
1. Login to trading terminal
2. Check overnight news and global markets
3. Review previous day's technical analysis
4. Identify sectors: Banking (strong), IT (weak), Auto (mixed)
5. Shortlist stocks for trading: HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, TCS, Maruti, INFY

**Margin Available:**
- Account balance: ₹10,00,000
- Intraday margin (5%): Can trade ₹20,00,000 worth (20x leverage)
- Strategy: Use 10x leverage (₹1,00,00,000 trading power)
- Conservative approach: Max ₹3,00,000 per trade

#### Morning Session: Pre-Open (9:00-9:15 AM)

**Priya's Trading Plan:**
```
Setup: HDFC Bank - Shooting star yesterday, support at ₹2,480
Trade 1: Buy on dip to ₹2,480, target ₹2,520, SL ₹2,470
Target P&L: ₹40 profit x 100 shares = ₹4,000 (Risk/Reward 1:4)

Setup: TCS - Above moving average, breakout imminent
Trade 2: Wait for opening breakout above ₹2,050
Entry: Breakout order at ₹2,055, Target ₹2,100, SL ₹2,020
Target P&L: ₹45 x 50 shares = ₹2,250
```

**Pre-open Analysis:**
- Nifty 50 futures up 80 points (positive open expected)
- Banking stocks expected to open strong
- IT selling visible (global weakness)

#### Market Open: 9:15-9:30 AM

**9:15 AM - Opening Call Auction:**
- Nifty opens at +90 points
- HDFC Bank opens at ₹2,505 (above support)
- Priya skips Trade 1 (missed dip)

**9:18 AM - TCS Breakout:**
- TCS breaks above ₹2,050
- Priya places order: "BUY 50 TCS @ 2,055 SL 2,020"
- Order executes at ₹2,052 (better than expected)
- Confirmation: 50 TCS bought @ ₹2,052, Amount: ₹1,02,600

**Trade Cost Analysis:**
- Brokerage: ₹1,02,600 x 0.05% = ₹51
- Transaction tax: ₹1,02,600 x 0.01% = ₹103
- Total cost: ₹154
- Break-even: Need ₹2,053.50 exit (₹1,500 total profit needed for profit)

#### Mid-Morning: 10:00-11:00 AM

**10:30 AM - TCS Position Update:**
- Price rises to ₹2,075 (+₹23)
- Profit so far: ₹23 x 50 = ₹1,150 (after commission)
- Priya holds for target ₹2,100

**10:45 AM - HDFC Bank Opportunity:**
- Price dips to ₹2,485 (support touch)
- Priya places: "BUY 100 HDFC @ 2,485 SL 2,470 Target 2,530"
- Order executes at ₹2,483
- Amount: ₹2,48,300
- Ratio: Risk ₹1,500 (100 x 13), Reward ₹4,700 (100 x 47)

**Running Positions:**
- TCS: +50 shares, +₹1,150 profit
- HDFC Bank: +100 shares, breakeven (just entered)

#### Late Morning: 11:30 AM-12:30 PM

**11:50 AM - Partial Booking:**
- TCS reaches ₹2,090 (close to target)
- Priya sells 30 shares @ ₹2,088
- Profit: (₹2,088 - ₹2,052) x 30 = ₹1,080 BOOKED
- Remaining: 20 TCS held for higher target

**12:15 PM - HDFC Bank Extends:**
- HDFC Bank rallies to ₹2,510 (↑₹27 from entry)
- Profit: ₹27 x 100 = ₹2,700
- Priya books 50% profit: Sell 50 shares @ ₹2,510
- Profit: ₹1,350 BOOKED
- Remaining: 50 shares held

**Net P&L Mid-day:**
- TCS profit booked: ₹1,080
- HDFC profit booked: ₹1,350
- Total booked: ₹2,430
- Remaining positions: 20 TCS, 50 HDFC Bank (floating)

#### Afternoon Session: 2:00-3:00 PM

**1:45 PM - Volatility Spike:**
- RBI policy announcement (hawkish stance)
- Banking stocks sell-off sharp
- HDFC Bank drops to ₹2,495 (-₹15 from highs)
- TCS rises to ₹2,110 (strong rally)

**2:00 PM - Final Exits:**
- Exit TCS remaining 20 @ ₹2,108: Profit ₹20 x (₹2,108 - ₹2,052) = ₹1,120
- Exit HDFC remaining 50 @ ₹2,495: Loss ₹50 x (₹2,495 - ₹2,483) = -₹600

**Final P&L for the Day:**

|-------|-------|------|-----|-----------|-------|

## PROCESS 3: DERIVATIVES TRADING - FUTURES HEDGING

### Scenario: Export Company Hedging Currency and Commodity Risk

**Company:** TechExports India Ltd  
**Business:** Software exports (USD receipts)  
**Challenge:** ₹80 lakh USD receivable in 3 months; worried about Rupee strengthening

#### Business Situation

**Current Details:**
- Expected USD inflow: ₹80 lakhs (assume ₹10 lakh monthly for 8 months)
- Current USD/INR rate: ₹82.50
- Expected rate (unhedged concern): Could appreciate to ₹80.00 (3% loss on 3 months)
- Revenue loss at ₹80/USD: ₹15 lakh (3% of total)

#### Hedging Decision

**Option 1: Do Nothing (Unhedged)**
- Pro: No cost if Rupee depreciates
- Con: Rupee appreciation → Revenue loss

**Option 2: Forward Contract** (Traditional, but less flexible)
- Fix rate at ₹82.25 for 3 months
- Cost: Lost upside if Rupee weakens
- Issue: Locked, cannot exit

**Option 3: Futures Hedging** (RECOMMENDED)
- Sell USD futures to lock cost
- Flexibility to cover partially
- Trading allowed; not locked

#### Futures Hedging Execution

**Setup:**
1. Open trading account at MCX (commodity exchange)
2. Complete KYC (same as equity)
3. Fund account with margin (₹10 lakh for 10 contracts)

**Futures Contract Details:**
- Exchange: MCX
- Product: USD/INR Futures
- Lot size: ₹1,00,000 (10,000 USD)
- Current price: ₹82.50 per USD
- Expiry: 3-month contract (March 2026 contract)
- Margin required: 2.5% (low margin for commodity currency)

**Hedging Strategy:**

**Step 1: Calculate Hedge Ratio**
- Expected USD receipt: ₹80 lakhs (80 lakh USD numerically)
- Futures contract: ₹1,00,000 per contract (10,000 USD equivalent)
- Contracts needed: 80 / 1 = 80 contracts
- Total margin: 80 x (₹1,00,000 x 2.5%) = ₹20,00,000
- Cost: Expensive (need ₹20 lakh margin)

**Decision: Hedge 50% of exposure**
- Sell 40 contracts (₹40 lakh notional hedge)
- Leave 50% unhedged for upside potential
- Margin required: ₹10,00,000

**Step 2: Place Futures Sell Order**

**Order Details:**
- Contract: USD-INR March 2026 Futures
- Action: SELL (lock rate for future dollars)
- Quantity: 40 contracts
- Price: ₹82.50 (current market rate)
- Order type: Limit order @ ₹82.48 (try for better rate)

**Execution:**
- Order matched at ₹82.49
- 40 contracts short-sold
- Hedged notional: ₹40 lakh
- Cost: Premium paid = Negligible (matched at near-spot)
- Margin blocked: ₹10,00,000

#### Monitoring Through 3 Months

**Scenario 1: Rupee Depreciation (Positive for Business)**

| Timeline | USD/INR Rate | Futures P&L | Business P&L | Net Effect |
|----------|-------------|------------|--------------|-----------|
| Month 0 (Entry) | ₹82.50 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| Month 1 | ₹83.50 (+₹1) | -₹40 lakh | +₹40 lakh | Flat |
| Month 2 | ₹84.00 (+₹1.50) | -₹60 lakh | +₹60 lakh | Flat |
| Month 3 Exit | ₹84.50 (+₹2) | -₹80 lakh | +₹80 lakh | Flat |

**Result:** Business protected against appreciation but lost upside. Net receipt locked at ₹82.49.

**Scenario 2: Rupee Appreciation (Negative for Business)**

| Timeline | USD/INR Rate | Futures P&L | Business P&L | Net Effect |
|----------|-------------|------------|--------------|-----------|
| Month 0 (Entry) | ₹82.50 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| Month 1 | ₹81.50 (-₹1) | +₹40 lakh | -₹40 lakh | Flat |
| Month 2 | ₹80.50 (-₹2) | +₹80 lakh | -₹80 lakh | Flat |
| Month 3 Exit | ₹80.00 (-₹2.50) | +₹100 lakh | -₹100 lakh | Flat |

**Result:** Futures profit offsets business loss. Hedging works!

#### End of Period Settlement

**Actual Scenario: USD/INR drops to ₹80.50**

**Business Side:**
- ₹80 lakh USD received → Valued at ₹80 x 80.50 lakh = ₹64.4 crore

**Futures Side:**
- Closed 40 contracts @ ₹80.50 (bought back to close short)
- Entry: -40 @ ₹82.49 (sold)
- Exit: +40 @ ₹80.50 (bought)
- P&L per contract: (₹82.49 - ₹80.50) x ₹1,00,000 = ₹1.99 x ₹1,00,000 = ₹1,99,000
- Total Futures profit: ₹1,99,000 x 40 = ₹79.6 lakh

**Total Receipt:**
- Unhedged portion (50%): ₹40 lakh at ₹80.50 = ₹32.2 crore
- Hedged portion (50%): ₹40 lakh locked at ₹82.49 = ₹32.996 crore
- **Total: ₹65.196 crore** (vs ₹64.4 crore unhedged = ₹79.6 lakh better!)

**Conclusion:**
- Hedging protected company from full impact of Rupee appreciation
- Locked ₹40 lakh portion at ₹82.49 rate (fixed)
- Kept ₹40 lakh unhedged (participated in 50% upside/downside)
- Net: Better than either full hedge or no hedge

## PROCESS 4: CORPORATE ACTIONS - DIVIDEND REINVESTMENT

### Scenario: Long-term Investor Receives Dividend

**Company:** HDFC Bank  
**Shares Held:** 500 shares @ ₹2,500 = ₹12,50,000  
**Announcement:** Dividend ₹28 per share (2-year high)

#### Timeline

**Day 1 (Announcement Date - Ex-Div Date):**
- HDFC Bank board announces ₹28 dividend
- Ex-dividend date: May 20, 2026
- Record date: May 22, 2026
- Payment date: June 5, 2026

**Status Check:**
- Your holding: 500 shares (purchased on April 1)
- Ownership before ex-date: YES → Eligible for dividend
- Amount due: 500 x ₹28 = ₹14,000

#### Process

**May 20 (Ex-Dividend Date):**
- Your holdings: Still 500 shares
- Stock price adjustment: Typically drops by dividend amount
- HDFC Bank closes @ ₹2,472 (₹28 lower)
- Your portfolio value: 500 x ₹2,472 = ₹12,36,000 (down ₹14,000)
- Net: Same value, but now in cash + shares

**May 22 (Record Date):**
- Transfer agent records your name for dividend eligibility
- No action needed

**June 5 (Payment Date):**
- Dividend ₹14,000 credited to your trading account
- Confirmation email received
- Status: CASH now available in your account

#### Decision: Reinvestment vs Withdrawal

**Option 1: Reinvest Dividend (Compounding)**
- Use ₹14,000 to buy more shares
- Current price: ₹2,480 (slightly recovered)
- Shares bought: ₹14,000 / ₹2,480 = 5.65 shares (fractional)
- New holding: 505.65 shares
- Strategy: **Recommended for long-term wealth creation**

**Execution:**
- Place buy order: "BUY 5.65 HDFC Bank @ ₹2,480"
- Most exchanges now allow fractional orders
- Settlement: T+1 (next day)
- New effective cost: (12,50,000 + 14,000) / 505.65 = ₹2,481.23 per share

**Benefits of Dividend Reinvestment:**
- Compound growth (Einstein's 8th wonder)
- Reduces average cost if buying at lower prices (dollar-cost averaging)
- Accumulates more shares over time
- Long-term wealth building

**Tax Implication:**
- Dividend reinvestment treated as NEW purchase
- Holding period starts fresh for reinvested shares
- Eventually long-term when held 1 year

**10-Year Projection (With Reinvestment):**
```
Year 0: 500 shares @ ₹2,500 = ₹12,50,000
Year 1: 505.65 shares (reinvested ₹14k dividend)
Year 2: ~512 shares (assuming 3% annual growth)
...
Year 10: ~630 shares (with dividend reinvestment compounding)
Value @ ₹4,500 (assumed appreciation): ₹28.35 lakh
Total Return: 126% over 10 years (8.3% annual CAGR)
```
