## 1. INTRODUCTION TO NSE

### 1.1 About National Stock Exchange

**Establishment:** November 3, 1994
**Location:** Exchange Plaza, Bandra-Kurla Complex, Bandra (East), Mumbai - 400051
**Recognition:** First fully automated stock exchange in India
**Regulatory Authority:** SEBI and Reserve Bank of India

**Mission:** Create an efficient, transparent, and safe securities market for all participants

**Core Functions:**
- Securities trading platform
- Risk management and surveillance
- Clearing and settlement services
- Market data distribution
- Training and capacity building

### 1.2 Market Statistics (As of 2026)

- **Listed Companies:** 2,000+ companies
- **Market Capitalization:** ₹300+ lakh crore
- **Average Daily Turnover:** ₹9,000+ crore (equity cash)
- **Active Trading Members:** 600+ brokers
- **Trading Terminals:** 50,000+ nationwide

### 1.3 NSE's Role in Indian Securities Market

NSE is the primary exchange for:
- Equity trading (shares)
- Derivative trading (futures, options)
- Debt trading (bonds, debentures)
- Currency trading (forex derivatives)
- Commodity derivatives
- Mutual fund trading
- Government securities trading

## 2. MARKET STRUCTURE AND SEGMENTS

### 2.1 Equity Market Segments

#### Capital Market (Equities)

**Main Board:**
- Bluechip companies with long track record
- Large market capitalization
- High liquidity and volumes
- Sector representation: IT, Banking, Auto, FMCG, Pharma, etc.

**Mid Cap and Small Cap Segments:**
- Growing companies with 2-10 years of operations
- Medium capitalization
- Lower volumes but growing interest
- Higher growth potential

**Indices:**
- **Nifty 50:** Top 50 liquid companies
- **Nifty Next 50:** Next 50 companies after Nifty 50
- **Nifty 100, Nifty 200:** Broader market indices
- **Sector Indices:** Nifty Bank, Nifty IT, Nifty Auto, Nifty Pharma, etc.
- **Thematic Indices:** Nifty Growth, Nifty Dividend, Nifty PSU Bank, etc.

#### Derivative Segment

**Index Derivatives:**
- Nifty 50 Futures and Options
- Nifty Bank Futures and Options
- Nifty Next 50 Options
- Nifty Midcap 50 Options

**Stock Derivatives:**
- Individual stock futures (100+ contracts)
- Individual stock options (100+ contracts)
- Strike prices: Every ₹100 for stocks, various intervals for indices
- Expiry: Weekly (Monday-Thursday), Monthly (last Thursday), Quarterly (last Thursday of quarter)

#### Debt Market

**Government Securities:**
- Central government bonds with 5, 10, 30 year tenures
- T-bills for short-term funding
- Auction through Reserve Bank of India

**Corporate Bonds:**
- Investment-grade corporate debentures
- High-yield bonds
- Convertible bonds
- Floating rate bonds

#### Currency Derivatives

**EUR/INR, GBP/INR, JPY/INR, USD/INR futures and options**

## 3. TRADING HOURS AND SESSIONS

### 3.1 Regular Trading Hours (Monday to Friday)

| Session | Time | Purpose |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Pre-Open** | 09:00 - 09:15 AM | Order entry, modification, cancellation |
| **Open Auction** | 09:15 - 09:15:30 AM | Price discovery through auction |
| **Continuous Trading** | 09:15:30 AM - 03:30 PM | Regular market trading |
| **Closing Auction** | 03:29 - 03:30 PM | Final price determination |
| **Post-Closing** | After 03:30 PM | ATS (After Trading Session) - limited orders |

### 3.2 Market Close and Holidays

**Market Holiday Closure:**
- Republic Day (January 26)
- Independence Day (August 15)
- Gandhi Jayanti (October 2)
- Christmas (December 25)
- Diwali, Holi (notification-based)
- Mahavir Jayanti, Guru Nanak Jayanti (as per notification)

**Half Days (Early Close at 01:00 PM):**
- Day before Independence Day
- Day after Diwali
- New Year Eve

### 3.3 Corporate Action Announcements

NSE announces ex-date, record date for:
- Dividend distribution
- Stock split/bonus
- Rights issue
- Demerger
- Scheme of arrangement

## 4. SETTLEMENT AND CLEARING

### 4.1 Settlement Cycle

**T+1 Settlement:**
- Trade executed on day T
- Settlement obligation on T+1 (next business day)
- Payment deadline: 3:00 PM on settlement day
- Securities delivery through depositories

**Settlement Process:**
1. Trade capture at exchange
2. Trade confirmation to broker
3. Broker netting of trades
4. Clearing corporation validation
5. Security and cash settlement

### 4.2 NSE Clearing Services

**National Securities Clearing Corporation (NSCC):**
- Central counterparty for all NSE transactions
- Guarantor of settlement through SGF
- Risk management through mark-to-market
- Real-time gross settlement (RTGS) for derivatives

**Clearing Parameters:**
- Position limit monitoring per member
- Intraday margin requirement
- End-of-day margin requirement
- Corporate action adjustments

### 4.3 Settlement Instructions

**Depository Participation:**
- Brokers use NSDL and CDSL for securities transfer
- Book entry transfer within T+1
- Optional physical share redemption
- IEPF mapping for unclaimed shares

**Payment Settlement:**
- RTGS through Reserve Bank of India
- Real-time funds transfer
- Automated reconciliation
- Failed transaction handling

- Delivery based settlement

- Price bands: +/- 10% for stocks, +/- 20% for specified stocks

#### Futures:
- Monthly, weekly expiries
- Standardized contract sizes
- Margined accounts
- Mark-to-market daily
- Settlement: Cash settlement for index, physical for stock

#### Options:
- Call and Put options
- In-the-money, At-the-money, Out-of-the-money strikes

### 5.3 Debt Market

- 5-year, 10-year, 30-year bonds

### 5.4 Commodity Derivatives

**NSE Commodity Derivatives (jointly with MCX):**
- Gold, Silver

## 6. TRADING RULES AND PROTOCOLS

### 6.1 Order Entry and Execution

**Order Parameters:**

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Valid Quantity** | Depends on security, minimum multiple |
| **Valid Price** | As per price band mechanism |
| **Order Duration** | GTC (Good-till-Cancel) up to 12 calendar days for equity |
| **Order Types** | Market, Limit, Stop-Loss, Bracket Orders |
| **Order Time** | Must be placed during trading hours |
| **Modification** | Allowed for un-executed orders during continuous trading |
| **Cancellation** | Free cancellation before execution |

### 6.2 Trading Bands and Circuit Breakers

**Circuit Breaker Framework:**

| Level | Index/Stock | Duration | Condition |
|-------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Level 1** | 10% movement | Half-minute halt | Halt in all derivatives |
| **Level 2** | 15% movement | Whole-minute halt | Halt in all derivatives |
| **Level 3** | 20% movement | Full market close | Full trading halt |

**Individual Stock Circuit Breaker:**
- 20% movement: Half-minute halt
- 25% movement: Whole-minute halt
- 30% movement: Full day closure (specified stocks)

### 6.3 Volatility Protection Measures

**Enhanced Volatility Monitoring:**
- Real-time price monitoring
- Unusual trading pattern detection
- Automatic trading suspension trigger
- Supervisor intervention protocols

**Volatility Threshold Measurement:**
- Standard deviation calculation
- Historical volatility vs implied volatility
- Bid-ask spread widening
- Volume spike detection

### 6.4 Price Band Mechanism

**For Most Securities:**
- Upper band: +10% from previous close
- Lower band: -10% from previous close
- No trading allowed outside bands

**For Specified Securities:**
- Upper band: +20% from previous close
- Lower band: -20% from previous close
- Applied to volatile/illiquid counters

**At Market Close:**
- Bands reset to previous close +/- 10% for next session
- Gap-up/gap-down openings allowed as per auction results

## 7. SURVEILLANCE AND RISK MANAGEMENT

### 7.1 Market Surveillance

**Surveillance Department:** Detects and reports potential violations

**Surveillance Techniques:**
- Real-time transaction monitoring
- Unusual trading pattern detection
- Order book depth analysis
- Large trade scrutiny
- Price manipulation detection

**Common Violations Detected:**
- Insider trading
- Front-running
- Layering (spoofing)
- Painting the tape
- Pump and dump schemes
- Wash trades
- Matched orders

### 7.2 Risk Management for Brokers

**Member Obligation:**
- Capital adequacy maintenance (7.5% minimum)
- Real-time margin monitoring
- Daily P&L settlement
- Risk limit framework
- Fraud detection procedures

**Broker Margin Requirements:**

| Product | Margin Type | Rate |
|---------|------------|------|
| **Equity Delivery** | Initial Margin | 20-25% |
| **Equity Intraday** | Initial Margin | 5-10% |
| **Equity Futures** | SPAN Margin | 10-15% |
| **Equity Options** | SPAN Margin | Calculated |
| **Index Derivatives** | SPAN Margin | 8-12% |

### 7.3 Default Management

**Member Default Protocol:**
1. Exchange issues margin call
2. 1-hour remedy period
3. If unremedied: Member suspension
4. Settlement Guarantee Fund (SGF) activated
5. Client positions transferred or squared-off
6. SGF compensation to affected clients

**Prevention Mechanisms:**
- Real-time member monitoring
- Daily member financials review
- Unexpected audit mechanisms
- Stressed market simulations

## 8. REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

### 8.1 Trading Member Registration

**Registration with NSE:**
- SEBI registration mandatory
- Exchange membership fee
- Capital adequacy certification
- Professional competency exam pass
- Insurance coverage: ₹10 crore+ for client protection

**Annual Compliance Certification:**
- Board approval for compliance
- Compliance officer certification
- External audit report
- Trading statistics report
- Grievance resolution report

### 8.2 Client Account Opening

**KYC Requirements:**
- PAN card mandatory
- Aadhaar linking (optional but recommended)
- Address proof (Passport, Aadhaar, Voter ID, etc.)
- Income and occupation details
- Risk profile assessment
- Politically Exposed Person (PEP) screening

**Trading Agreement Clauses:**
- Order placement and execution procedure
- Margin requirements
- Settlement and delivery procedures
- Liability and dispute resolution
- Risk acknowledgment
- Grievance redressal mechanism

### 8.3 Reporting Requirements

**NSE Member Reporting:**
- Daily member report by 12:00 noon (T+1)
- Weekly audit report by Friday
- Monthly compliance certificate by 15th
- Quarterly trading statistics by 30th
- Annual report filing

**Investor Protection Requirements:**
- Separate client account for funds and securities
- Regular reconciliation
- Quarterly statement to clients
- Prompt grievance response
