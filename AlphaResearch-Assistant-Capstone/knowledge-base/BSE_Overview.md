## 1. INTRODUCTION AND HISTORY

### 1.1 About BSE

**Foundation:** Founded on July 9, 1875 as "The Native Share & Stock Brokers' Association"

**Key Milestones:**
- 1957: Renamed to Bombay Stock Exchange
- 1995: First demutual stock exchange in Asia
- 2000: Introduction of online trading
- 2017: Introduction of BSE Emerge platform for SMEs
- 2026: Technology upgrade with AI-driven surveillance

**Current Status:**
- India's oldest and third-largest stock exchange
- Headquarters: Dalal Street, Mumbai
- SEBI regulated entity
- Clearing: NSCC Limited, Depository: NSDL and CDSL

### 1.2 Market Characteristics

- **Listed Companies:** 5,000+ (highest in Asia)
- **Market Capitalization:** ₹280+ lakh crore
- **Daily Turnover:** ₹8,000+ crore (equity)
- **Registered Members:** 500+ brokers
- **Investor Base:** 5+ million active investors

## 2. MARKET SEGMENTS

### 2.1 Main Board (BSE Equity)

**Segment Code:** BSE

**Listing Requirements:**
- Minimum paid-up capital: ₹10 lakh
- Minimum market capitalization on listing: ₹25 crore
- 3-year track record with pre-tax profit
- Minimum public shareholding: 25%
- Eligibility for various index inclusions

**Company Categories:**
- **A Group:** Highly liquid, large-cap companies
- **B Group:** Medium and small cap companies
- **Z Group:** Companies with irregular trading or compliance issues

### 2.2 BSE Emerge (formerly MES - Main Board SME)

**Purpose:** Enabling SMEs and Mid-sized enterprises to access capital markets

**Eligibility:**
- Minimum paid-up capital: ₹1 crore
- Minimum pre-tax profit: ₹50 lakh (for 2 out of last 3 years)
- No minimum market cap requirement
- Relaxed disclosure requirements

**Features:**
- Lower listing cost than main board
- Simplified IPO process
- Quick listing (2-3 months)
- Access to SME funding opportunities
- Graduation path to main board

**Trading:**
- Lot size: ₹1 lakh denominations
- Lower liquidity compared to main board
- Standard trading hours
- T+1 settlement

### 2.3 BSE Debt Market

**Debt Products:**
- Government securities (Treasury bills and bonds)
- Corporate debentures and bonds
- Structured products
- Preference shares

**Features:**
- Dematerialized securities
- Electronic order matching
- Transparent pricing
- High volume liquidity in government securities

## 3. TRADING MECHANISM

### 3.1 Trading Hours

| Session | Time | Purpose |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Pre-Open** | 09:15 - 09:25 AM | Order entry and modifications |
| **Opening Call Auction** | 09:25 - 09:30 AM | Price discovery and opening |
| **Continuous Trading** | 09:30 AM - 03:30 PM | Regular trading session |
| **Closing Auction** | 03:29 - 03:30 PM | Closing price determination |

### 3.2 Order Types

**Available Orders:**
1. **Market Orders** - Immediate execution at best available price
2. **Limit Orders** - Execute at specified price or better
3. **Stop-Loss Orders** - Triggered at specified price
4. **Good-Till-Date (GTD)** - Valid for specified number of days
5. **Good-Till-Cancel (GTC)** - Remain active until cancelled
6. **Immediate-or-Cancel (IOC)** - Execute immediately or cancel
7. **All-or-Nothing (AON)** - Full quantity or no execution

**Order Restrictions:**
- Price bands: +/- 10% for most securities, +/- 20% for specified securities
- Quantity restrictions for new listings
- Corporate action related order restrictions

### 3.3 Settlement Cycle

**T+1 Settlement:**
- Trades executed on day T
- Settlement on T+1 (next business day)
- Payment deadline: 3:30 PM on settlement day
- Securities delivery through depositories

**Settlement Components:**
1. Trade confirmation within 30 seconds
2. Clearing house position netting
3. Margin calculation and call
4. RTGS fund transfer
5. Security delivery instruction
6. Depository settlement

## 5. SETTLEMENT FRAMEWORK

### 5.1 BSE Clearing Services

**Clearing House Functions:**
- Central counterparty clearing
- Risk management for all trades
- Margin collection and management
- Settlement guarantee through SGF

**Clearing Process:**
1. Trade receipt and validation
2. Netting of bilateral trades
3. Settlement obligation generation
4. Margin computation
5. Penalty calculation for failures
6. Settlement instruction transmission

### 5.2 Margin Requirements

**For Delivery Trades:**
- Initial margin: 20-30% (varies by stock)
- Maintenance margin: 80% of initial
- Additional margin for corporate actions

**For Intraday Trades:**
- Initial margin: 5-10% (varies by stock)
- Mark-to-market margin calls
- Square-off before market close

**For Derivatives:**
- SPAN margin calculated
- Volatility-adjusted
- Daily collection and settlement

### 5.3 Settlement Guarantees

**Settlement Guarantee Fund (SGF):**
- Protects clients against broker default
- Coverage: ₹5 crore per investor account
- Managed by NSCC
- Automatic compensation on broker failure

**Settlement Failures:**
- T+1 settlement strictly enforced
- Penalties for failed settlements
- Auto-square-off mechanism activated
- Broker responsible for remediation

## 6. SURVEILLANCE AND COMPLIANCE

### 6.1 Market Surveillance

**Surveillance Functions:**
- Real-time trade monitoring
- Price manipulation detection
- Volume spike analysis
- Insider trading identification
- Unauthorized trading prevention

**Surveillance Tools:**
- Artificial intelligence for pattern detection
- Historical analysis comparison
- Unusual trading activity flagging
- Corporate action impact assessment

### 6.2 Circuit Breakers

**Price Circuit Breaker Mechanism:**
- Level 1 (10%): 30-minute halt
- Level 2 (15%): 1-hour halt
- Level 3 (20%): Full day closure

**Individual Stock Circuit Breakers:**
- Applicable to specified volatile stocks
- Same percentage and timing as index
- Automatic halt and resumption

### 6.3 Compliance for Members

**Continuous Compliance Requirements:**
- Capital adequacy: Minimum 7.5%
- Real-time position monitoring
- Daily report filing
- Quarterly audit and certification
- Annual compliance statement

**Client Protection Requirements:**
- Segregated client accounts
- Regular client statements
- Prompt grievance redressal
- Dispute resolution mechanism
- Insurance coverage maintenance

## 7. BSE EMERGE - SME PLATFORM

### 7.1 Purpose and Features

**Objectives:**
- Provide funding opportunity for SMEs and mid-sized companies
- Bridge the gap between private and public markets
- Facilitate growth capital raising
- Create a path to main board listing

**Key Advantages:**
- Lower compliance cost
- Faster listing process
- Simplified regulatory requirements
- Access to institutional investors

### 7.2 Listing Requirements for BSE Emerge

**Financial Eligibility:**
- Minimum paid-up capital: ₹1 crore
- Minimum 2 years of operation
- Pre-tax profit ₹50 lakh for last 2 years
- No minimum market cap requirement

**Structural Eligibility:**
- Proper corporate governance
- Board composition norms
- Disclosure requirements
- Annual reporting framework

### 7.3 Trading and Settlement

**Order Placement:**
- Lot size: ₹1 lakh minimum
- Standard limit and market orders
- Electronic order matching
- Real-time order status

**Settlement Details:**
- T+1 settlement (similar to main board)
- Demat account mandatory
- SGF protection for investors
- Lower trading volumes
