## 1. INTRODUCTION TO SEBI

### 1.1 What is SEBI?

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) is the regulatory authority for the securities market in India. Established on April 12, 1992, under the SEBI Act, 1992, SEBI is responsible for:

- **Protecting investor interests**
- **Promoting market development**
- **Regulating market intermediaries**
- **Enforcing securities laws**

### 1.2 SEBI's Regulatory Scope

SEBI has jurisdiction over:
- Stock exchanges (NSE, BSE, MSEI)
- Intermediaries (brokers, sub-brokers, dealers)
- Collective investment schemes (mutual funds, ETFs)
- Venture capital funds
- Credit rating agencies
- Depository services
- Portfolio managers and investment advisors

## 2. REGULATORY FRAMEWORK

### 2.1 Key Legislation

#### Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992
- Provides constitutional framework for SEBI
- Defines SEBI's powers and functions
- Section 4: Establishment and constitution of SEBI
- Section 15-18: Delegation and coordination with other authorities

#### Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956
- Governs stock exchanges and brokers
- Regulates securities contracts and their transactions
- Key provisions:
  - Section 18: Prohibits operations of unregulated exchanges
  - Section 21: Listing requirements for stock exchanges
  - Section 23: Recognition of stock exchanges by SEBI

#### Depositories Act, 1996
- Governs the framework for electronic holding and transfer of securities
- Allows book-entry transfers instead of physical securities
- SEBI recognizes depositories: NSDL and CDSL
- Provides for creation of depository participant (DP) network

#### Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002
- Anti-money laundering compliance
- Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements
- Suspicious transaction reporting (STR)
- Beneficial ownership disclosure

### 2.2 SEBI Regulations

#### SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices) Regulations, 2003
- **Insider Trading**: Trading on material non-public information is prohibited
  - Insider = connected person with access to unpublished price-sensitive information (UPSI)
  - Penalty: Up to ₹25 crore or 5 years imprisonment
  - Duty to maintain confidentiality of UPSI
  
- **Price Manipulation**: Any action to artificially inflate/deflate security prices
  - Punishable by imprisonment up to 3 years and/or fine
  - Market surveillance mechanisms to detect
  
- **False Disclosures**: Publishing misleading information
  - Directors and key managerial personnel liable
  - Penalty: Up to ₹10 crore

#### SEBI (Issue of Capital and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2018
- Primary market regulations for IPOs
- Requirement for public and rights offers
- Disclosure requirements: RHP, DRH, DRHP
- Underwriting and allotment procedures
- Minimum public shareholding (25%) requirements

#### SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015
- Companies must follow continuous disclosure norms
- Half-yearly, annual, quarterly reporting requirements
- Material event and information disclosure within 30 minutes
- Related party transaction disclosures
- Segment-wise reporting
- Compliance certificate from auditor

#### SEBI (Prohibition of Fraudulent and Unfair Trade Practices) Amendment Regulations, 2018
- Expanded insider trading definition
- Covers derivatives and structured products
- Extends to persons trading on behalf of insiders

### 2.3 SEBI Circulars and Guidelines

SEBI issues circulars, guidelines, and master circulars for:
- Custody requirements for securities
- Broking and trading operations
- Margin and collateral management
- Settlement procedures
- Risk management framework

## 3. MARKET CONDUCT RULES

### 3.1 Insider Trading Provisions

**Definition of UPSI (Unpublished Price Sensitive Information):**
- Earnings and financial results
- Mergers, acquisitions, demergers
- Asset sales or purchases
- Changes in management or board composition
- Dividend announcements
- Changes in auditors
- Any information likely to materially affect security prices

**Prohibited Activities:**
1. Trading in securities while in possession of UPSI
2. Tipping (communicating UPSI to others)
3. Trading through family members or nominees
4. Front-running on client information

**Compliance Requirement:**
- Companies must maintain "Trading Window" - restrict trading 48 hours before quarterly results
- Maintain insider list and conduct trading restrictions
- Annual certification of compliance

### 3.2 Code of Conduct for Brokers and Sub-brokers

**SEBI Registration Requirements:**
- Brokers must be members of recognized stock exchange
- Capital adequacy ratio: Minimum 7.5% (can be higher per exchange rules)
- Mandatory professional competency examination
- Insurance coverage for clients' securities and cash

**Broker Responsibilities:**
- Maintain segregated client accounts
- Implement margin requirements
- Real-time reporting to exchange
- Regular audit and compliance certification
- Protection against unauthorized transactions

**Sub-broker Registration:**
- Sponsored by stockbroker with SEBI approval
- Similar regulatory oversight as broker
- Limited trading desk operations
- Client-facing operations (order taking, clearing)

### 3.3 Stock Exchange Surveillance

**NSE and BSE conduct continuous surveillance:**
- Real-time price and volume monitoring
- Unusual trading pattern detection
- Corporate action notifications
- Trading halt triggers
- Circuit breaker mechanisms

**NSE Trading Rules:**
- Circuit breaker triggers at 10%, 15%, 20% movement in index
- Individual stock circuit breakers
- Half-minute and whole-minute circuit breaks
- Mandatory trading halt procedures

## 4. INVESTOR PROTECTION

### 4.1 Investor Education and Protection Fund (IEPF)

**Purpose:**
- Protects unclaimed shares and debentures
- Manages investor grievance funds
- Conducts investor awareness programs

**When IEPF Takes Over:**
- Unclaimed dividends after 7 years
- Unclaimed shares with no financial activity
- Debenture redemption amounts

### 4.2 Settlement Guarantee Fund (SGF)

**Who Manages:** National Securities Clearing Corporation (NSCC)

**Coverage:**
- Protects investors against broker default
- Covers up to ₹5 crore per investor account (as of 2026)
- Limited to cash losses, not securities losses
- Automatic claim filing by exchange

**Claim Process:**
1. Broker default declared by exchange
2. NSCC identifies affected clients
3. Automatic credit to investor accounts
4. Claim settlement within 90 days

### 4.3 Grievance Redressal Mechanism

**SEBI Investor Grievance Redressal Portal:**
- Online complaint filing
- Tracking system for complaint status
- Resolution within 21 days for most complaints
- Ombudsman appeal available

**Stock Exchange Level:**
- Exchange grievance cell
- Fast-track resolution (5-7 days)
- Arbitration services

## 5. TRADING RULES AND REGULATIONS

### 5.1 Order Types and Trading Mechanisms

**Normal Market (NM) Segment:**
- T+1 Settlement (trade date + 1 business day)
- Regular trading hours: 9:15 AM - 3:30 PM
- Pre-open session: 9:00 AM - 9:15 AM
- Post-close trading window (if applicable)

**Order Types:**
- Market orders: Immediate execution at market price
- Limit orders: Execute at specified price or better
- Stop-loss orders: Trigger at specified price, then market order
- Bracket orders: Limit order with attached stop-loss and target
- Good-till-day (GTD): Execute within trading day
- Good-till-cancel (GTC): Remain active until manual cancellation
- All-or-nothing (AON): Execute full quantity or not at all
- Immediate-or-cancel (IOC): Execute available quantity, cancel remainder

**Order Duration:**
- Default: Good-till-day (until market close)
- Weekend and holidays: Orders automatically cancelled
- Margin requirements remain active during order holding period

### 5.2 Margin and Collateral Requirements

**Initial Margin (IM):**
- Broker requirement before order execution
- Typical rates: 20-25% of transaction value for equity delivery
- 5-10% for equity intraday trading
- Higher for volatile stocks and derivatives

**Maintenance Margin:**
- Minimum margin required after order execution
- Typically 80% of initial margin
- Varies by exchange and product

**Mark-to-market (M2M):**
- Daily revaluation of positions
- If M2M loss > threshold, additional margin called
- Broker can force liquidation if margin not met within specified time

**Collateral Accepted:**
- Cash (primary)
- Government securities
- Approved mutual fund units
- Pledged securities
- Fixed deposits from approved banks

### 5.3 Position Limits

**Individual Stock Limits:**
- Open interest limits: Prevent monopolistic practices
- Varies by liquidity and float
- NSE enforces position monitoring

**Derivative Limits:**
- Futures open interest caps
- Options open interest caps
- Index and stock-specific limits
- Client-wise limits for brokers

## 6. PENALTIES AND COMPLIANCE

### 6.1 Civil Penalties

**SEBI Enforcement Department:**
- Amount: Up to ₹10 crore per violation
- Recovered for violation of securities law
- Public interest determination

**Examples:**
- Insider trading: ₹5-25 crore
- Market manipulation: ₹10 crore
- Misleading disclosures: ₹10 crore
- Fraudulent trades: ₹50 lakh - ₹5 crore

### 6.2 Criminal Penalties

**Imprisonment and Fines:**
- Insider trading: Up to 5 years and ₹100 crore fine
- Fraud: Up to 10 years and ₹100 crore fine
- False disclosures: Up to 3 years and ₹25 crore fine

**Criminal Prosecution:**
- Through Serious Fraud Investigation Office (SFIO)
- In coordination with CBI/ED
- State police involvement for organized violations

### 6.3 Administrative Actions

**Cease and Desist:**
- Immediate order to stop illegal activity
- Freezing of trading accounts
- Securities cancellation orders

**Revocation of License:**
- Broker/sub-broker deregistration
- Mutual fund scheme closure
- Portfolio manager suspension

### 6.4 Compliance Certification

**Mandatory Annual Compliance:**
- Board approval for compliance policy
- Certification by compliance officer
- External auditor verification
- Filing with SEBI by specified dates

**Quarterly Compliance:**
- Trading statistics reporting
- Margin utilization reports
- Grievance resolution status
- Settlement and reconciliation reports
