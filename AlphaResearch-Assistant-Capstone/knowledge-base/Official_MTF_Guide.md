# Official Guide: Margin Trading Facility (MTF)

**Status:** Official web-enriched reference note  
**Primary Sources:** SEBI circulars and NSE member FAQ pages  
**Last Curated:** 2026-05-30

## What is MTF?

Margin Trading Facility (MTF) is a facility where an eligible stock broker funds part of a client's purchase of eligible securities, while the client provides prescribed margin. It is used for buying shares or eligible securities with broker funding, subject to SEBI and exchange rules.

**Official source context:**
- SEBI stock broker FAQ describes MTF as buying shares and securities by paying a margin.
- NSE's MTF FAQ explains member eligibility, client consent, eligible securities, margin collection, pledge/re-pledge, and reporting requirements.

## Broker Eligibility

Only eligible trading members can offer MTF to clients.

Key controls:
- The trading member must be a corporate member.
- NSE states that a corporate member must have net worth of at least Rs. 3.00 crore, computed under Schedule VI of SEBI Stock Brokers Regulations, 1992.
- The member must obtain prior approval from the exchange before offering MTF.
- Approval is sought through the exchange member/compliance portal with required documents such as board resolution and auditor certificate on net worth.

**Source:** NSE Members FAQ on Margin Trading Facility.

## Client Consent and Account Treatment

Before offering MTF to a client:

- The member must obtain client consent in writing or through an irrefutable electronic method.
- Rights, obligations, terms, and conditions must be communicated to the client.
- A client should have one Unique Client Code (UCC) per member.
- MTF and non-MTF positions should be segregated in the broker's back office even if mapped to the same UCC.

**Source:** NSE Members FAQ on Margin Trading Facility.

## Eligible Securities

Eligible securities for MTF are restricted.

SEBI and NSE guidance indicates:
- Equity shares and units of Equity ETFs classified as Group I securities are eligible.
- Group I securities are liquid securities that satisfy trading frequency and impact cost criteria.
- NSE clarifies that the Group I list is maintained and updated through objective liquidity criteria.

**Sources:**
- SEBI Master Circular for Stock Brokers.
- NSE Members FAQ on Margin Trading Facility.
- NSE/BSE clarification on margin funding media reports.

## Initial Margin and Maintenance Margin

To avail MTF, the client must provide prescribed initial margin.

Key points:
- Initial margin may be in cash, cash equivalent, or eligible Group I equity shares / Group I Equity ETFs with applicable haircut.
- Once a trade is identified as an MTF trade, members must collect upfront margin as per SEBI/exchange MTF guidelines, or the minimum cash-market margin requirement of 20% of transaction value, whichever is higher.
- Maintenance margin must be maintained for open funded positions as prescribed.
- Mark-to-market margin is required where market value of funded stock is less than funded cost.

**Sources:**
- NSE Members FAQ on Margin Trading Facility.
- SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-2/P/CIR/2024/118.

## Collateral, Pledge, and Re-Pledge

MTF collateral must be handled under prescribed pledge/re-pledge mechanisms.

Key controls:
- Collateral securities under MTF should be held by way of pledge.
- Securities pledged as collateral for MTF may be kept under the client securities margin pledge account, but the broker must record MTF collateral distinctly in MTF books.
- Members may re-pledge securities pledged by clients as MTF margin collateral with the clearing corporation or clearing member, as allowed under applicable rules.

**Source:** NSE Members FAQ on Margin Trading Facility.

## Funded Securities as Maintenance Margin

SEBI issued a circular on September 11, 2024 allowing securities funded through cash collateral as maintenance margin for MTF, subject to conditions.

Operational implication:
- Where client cash collateral used for MTF margin is subsequently used for pay-in, and funded stock is received from the clearing corporation, the funded stock may be treated as maintenance margin subject to prescribed margin rules.

**Source:** SEBI Circular SEBI/HO/MRD/MRD-PoD-2/P/CIR/2024/118 dated September 11, 2024.

## Reporting Requirements

MTF has daily reporting requirements.

NSE guidance states:
- Once a member starts offering MTF, exposure details must be reported to the exchange on or before 6:00 PM of the following trading day.
- Reporting is mandatory even if no MTF is provided on a particular trading day until the facility is formally withdrawn.
- Non-reporting of the daily MTF file is treated as non-compliance and may attract disciplinary action.

**Source:** NSE Members FAQ on Margin Trading Facility.

## Practical Broker Checklist

Before offering MTF:

- Confirm exchange approval for MTF.
- Validate net worth and eligibility.
- Obtain client consent and disclose rights/obligations.
- Ensure eligible securities are Group I securities / eligible ETFs.
- Collect prescribed upfront margin.
- Maintain funded-stock and collateral records in MTF books.
- Monitor maintenance margin and MTM requirements.
- Report MTF exposure to the exchange by the required deadline.
- Keep MTF and non-MTF client records segregated in back office.
- Track circular updates from SEBI and exchanges.
